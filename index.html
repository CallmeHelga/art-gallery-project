The Story of The Art Gallery

Hidden behind grand iron gates and timeworn stone walls, The Art Gallery is a sanctuary for the masterpieces of the past. Founded in 1872, it was established by an ambitious collector who dedicated his life to preserving the rarest works of art from past centuries.
Originally a private collection, the gallery housed lost treasures from the Renaissance, Baroque, and Romantic eras, featuring works by Caravaggio, Rembrandt, and Goya. Over the decades, it expanded into a public institution, opening its doors to those who seek to experience the untouched beauty of history.
Today, The Art Gallery remains a haven for art historians, collectors, and dreamers, displaying original masterpieces, restored rarities, and never-before-seen sketches from the greatest minds in art. The collection continues to grow, as art historians and experts work tirelessly to uncover lost masterpieces, acquire rare pieces from private collections, and restore long-forgotten works to their former glory.
Every painting, every sculpture whispers a tale from centuries past, waiting to be discovered. Step inside and walk through history—where art is not just displayed but relived.

Exhibitions

Caravaggio: Light and Shadow

March 15 - April 22

Step into the dramatic world of Caravaggio, the master of chiaroscuro, where light and darkness collide to create breathtaking intensity. This exhibition showcases some of his most iconic works, revealing the raw emotions, striking contrasts, and lifelike details that revolutionized Baroque.

From the “Saint Jerome Writing” to the powerful “The Calling of Saint Matthew”, each painting tells a story of passion, faith, and human struggle. Caravaggio’s revolutionary use of light brings his figures to life, making them seem almost tangible, their gazes piercing through centuries. Experience the genius of a rebellious artist who defied tradition, embraced realism, and forever changed the course of art history.

Vermeer: The Art of Light and Silence

April 25 - May 03

Step into the serene world of Johannes Vermeer, the Dutch master of light, intimacy, and quiet storytelling. This exhibition invites you to explore the delicate beauty of his paintings, where everyday moments are transformed into timeless masterpieces.
From the enigmatic gaze of “Girl with a Pearl Earring” to the soft daylight illuminating “View of Delft”, Vermeer’s works capture fleeting instants of life with astonishing precision. His mastery of light creates an almost dreamlike atmosphere, where every brushstroke whispers a story of stillness and contemplation.
Experience the magic of Vermeer’s art—a world where silence speaks, light dances on surfaces, and the simplest gestures become profound.

Goya: Visions of Power and Madness

May 10 - May 25

Enter the enigmatic world of Francisco Goya, where history, imagination, and raw emotion collide. This exhibition unveils “Hannibal the Conqueror Viewing Italy for the First Time from the Alps”, a striking yet lesser-known masterpiece by the Spanish visionary. Unlike his later dark and nightmarish works, this painting captures a moment of anticipation and grandeur —the legendary Carthaginian general gazing upon the lands he would soon invade. Yet, beneath the triumph lies an eerie sense of foreboding, a prelude to the chaos and destruction that would follow. Goya’s mastery of light, movement, and psychological depth transforms this historical scene into something far more than a simple military triumph. It is a reflection on ambition, fate, and the power struggles that shape history.
Step into Goya’s vision—where the past feels unsettlingly alive, and every brushstroke whispers of hidden truths.
